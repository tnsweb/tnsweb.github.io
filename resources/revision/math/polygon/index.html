Math Revision - Polygons

Definition of a polygon: A closed figure composed of straight sides with no sides overlapping.
Definition of a regpar polygon: A polygon with all the sides the same length.
No. of diagonals in a polygon where no. of sides=n = n(n-3) ÷ 2
No. of diagonals from a vertex in a polygon where no. of sides=n = n - 2
Exterior angle of a polygon where no. of sides=n = 360 ÷ n

Names of 4-sided polygons (quadrilaterals):

Trapezium

Exactly one pair of parallel sides
If non-parallel sides have equal length, it is an isoceles trapezium
If there is a right angle, it is a right trapezium

Rectangle

2 pairs of parallel sides
All angles are right angles
Inherits from Parallelogram

Rhombus

All sides the same
Consecutive angles are supplementary
Diagonals perpendicparly bisect each other
Inherits from Parallelogram

Parallelogram

Opposite sides are congruent and parallel
Opposite angles are equal
Diagonals bisect each other

Kite

2 pairs of adjacent sides are congruent
1 pair of opposite corners are equal
Diagonals intersect at right angles
1 diagonal bisects the other

Square

Inherits from Parallelogram, Rectangle, Rhombus, Kite
